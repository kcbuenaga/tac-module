# Source Types and Required Metadata

**Purpose:** Define source types and their required metadata fields for citation formatting.

---

## Common Source Types

### 1. Book

**Category:** Print/Digital Publication

**Required Metadata:**
- Author(s) - Full name(s)
- Year - Publication year
- Title - Complete book title (and subtitle if applicable)
- Publisher - Publishing company
- Location - City of publication

**Optional Metadata:**
- Edition - If not first edition (2nd ed., 3rd ed., etc.)
- Volume - For multi-volume works
- Pages - Total pages (for reference)
- ISBN - International Standard Book Number
- Translator - If translated work
- DOI - Digital Object Identifier (for e-books)
- URL - For e-books or online access

**Typical Indicators:**
- Contains chapters
- Published by a publisher (not periodical)
- Has ISBN
- Usually longer than 50 pages

---

### 2. Journal Article

**Category:** Scholarly Publication

**Required Metadata:**
- Author(s) - Article authors
- Year - Publication year
- Article Title - Title of the specific article
- Journal Name - Name of the academic journal
- Volume - Volume number
- Issue - Issue number (optional in some styles)
- Pages - Page range (start-end)

**Optional Metadata:**
- DOI - Highly recommended for academic articles
- ISSN - International Standard Serial Number
- Month - Publication month
- URL - If accessed online

**Typical Indicators:**
- Published in academic/peer-reviewed journal
- Has volume and issue numbers
- Page numbers like "v. 25, n. 3, p. 45-62"
- Often has DOI
- Shorter than books (usually 5-40 pages)

---

### 3. Website / Web Page

**Category:** Online Resource

**Required Metadata:**
- Author - Individual author OR organization
- Year - Publication year OR access year if no publication date
- Title - Page title or document title
- Website Name - Name of the website (if different from author)
- URL - Full web address
- Access Date - Date when content was accessed

**Optional Metadata:**
- Publication Date - Specific date if available (beyond year)
- Organization - If different from author

**Typical Indicators:**
- Accessed via web browser
- Has URL (http:// or https://)
- Not a formal academic publication
- May not have clear publication date

---

### 4. Newspaper Article

**Category:** News/Media Publication

**Required Metadata:**
- Author - Article author (if byline exists)
- Date - Full date (Year, Month, Day)
- Article Title - Headline
- Newspaper Name - Name of newspaper
- Pages - Page number or section

**Optional Metadata:**
- Section - Section name (e.g., Business, Sports)
- City - City of publication
- URL - If accessed online

**Typical Indicators:**
- Published daily or weekly
- Has specific date (not just year)
- Has sections (A, B, Sports, Business, etc.)
- Journalistic rather than academic

---

### 5. Thesis / Dissertation

**Category:** Academic Research

**Required Metadata:**
- Author - Researcher name
- Year - Year of defense/submission
- Title - Complete thesis title
- Degree Type - Master's thesis, Doctoral dissertation, etc.
- Institution - University or institution
- Department/Program - Academic department or program
- City - Location of institution

**Optional Metadata:**
- Number of Pages - Total pages/sheets
- Advisor - Thesis advisor name
- URL - If available in digital repository
- Database - Name of repository (e.g., USP Digital Library)

**Typical Indicators:**
- Contains "Master's thesis" or "Doctoral dissertation"
- From a university
- Research project for degree
- Often 100+ pages

---

### 6. Conference Paper / Proceedings

**Category:** Academic Conference

**Required Metadata:**
- Author(s) - Paper authors
- Year - Conference year
- Paper Title - Title of the presentation/paper
- Conference Name - Full name of conference
- Location - City and country of conference
- Pages - Page range in proceedings (if published)

**Optional Metadata:**
- Date Range - Specific dates of conference
- Publisher - If proceedings published
- DOI - If published proceedings have DOI
- Type - [Conference presentation], [Poster presentation], etc.

**Typical Indicators:**
- Presented at a conference/symposium
- Has conference name and location
- May be published in proceedings
- Often shorter than journal articles

---

### 7. E-book

**Category:** Digital Book

**Required Metadata:**
- Same as Book (see #1)
- PLUS: DOI or URL
- Platform - E-book platform (Kindle, Google Books, etc.) if relevant

**Note:** Format is essentially same as print book, but include digital access information.

**Typical Indicators:**
- Same as book but accessed digitally
- May have DOI
- Often from platforms like Kindle, Google Books, library databases

---

### 8. Report / Technical Document

**Category:** Institutional/Government Document

**Required Metadata:**
- Author - Organization or agency
- Year - Publication year
- Title - Report title
- Location - City
- Publisher - Publishing organization (if different from author)

**Optional Metadata:**
- Report Number - Official report number
- Pages - Total pages
- Series - If part of series
- URL - If available online

**Typical Indicators:**
- Published by government, corporation, or organization
- May have report number
- Often technical or policy-focused
- Institutional rather than individual authorship

---

### 9. Video / Podcast / Multimedia

**Category:** Digital Media

**Required Metadata:**
- Creator(s) - Director, producer, or channel name
- Year - Publication/release year
- Title - Video/episode title
- Platform - YouTube, Spotify, etc.
- URL - Link to content

**Optional Metadata:**
- Series Name - If part of series
- Duration - Length of content
- Access Date - When accessed

**Typical Indicators:**
- Accessed via YouTube, Vimeo, Spotify, etc.
- Video or audio format
- May be educational or entertainment

---

### 10. Social Media Post

**Category:** Social Media

**Required Metadata:**
- Author - Username or real name
- Date - Full date of post
- Content Description - First 20 words or description
- Platform - Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, etc.
- URL - Direct link to post

**Note:** Generally not acceptable for academic work (warn in Step 3).

---

### 11. Blog Post

**Category:** Online Publication

**Required Metadata:**
- Author - Blog author
- Date - Publication date
- Post Title - Title of blog post
- Blog Name - Name of blog (if different from author)
- URL - Link to post

**Optional Metadata:**
- Platform - WordPress, Medium, etc.
- Access Date

**Typical Indicators:**
- Personal or organizational blog
- Less formal than journal article
- May or may not be acceptable (depends on author credentials)

---

### 12. Legislation / Legal Document

**Category:** Legal Source

**Required Metadata:**
- Title - Name of law/statute
- Number - Law number
- Date - Enactment date
- Jurisdiction - Country, state, or region
- Official Publication - Where published

**Typical Indicators:**
- Government legislation
- Has official number (e.g., Lei nº 9.394)
- Published in official gazette

---

## Metadata Field Definitions

### Author
- Individual: Full name (First Last)
- Multiple: All authors listed
- Organization: Official name of organization
- Anonymous: Use "Anonymous" if truly no author

### Title
- Exact title as it appears on source
- Include subtitle if present
- Capitalization follows citation standard (ABNT: capitalize normally, APA: sentence case)

### Year
- 4-digit publication year
- If no year: use "n.d." (no date) or access year

### Publisher
- Official name of publishing company
- For academic work: University press name
- For websites: May be same as author

### URL
- Complete web address starting with http:// or https://
- Should be direct link to source
- Check that link is not temporary or behind paywall

### DOI
- Format: 10.XXXX/identifier
- Preferred over URL when available
- Permanent identifier for academic articles

### Pages
- Format depends on citation standard
- ABNT: p. 45-62
- APA: 45-62 or pp. 45-62 for in-text

---

## Source Type Detection Tips

**Has volume and issue numbers?** → Likely journal article
**Has publisher and ISBN?** → Likely book
**Has DOI?** → Likely journal article or e-book
**Has conference name?** → Conference paper
**Has university name + "thesis" or "dissertation"?** → Thesis/dissertation
**Has URL but no formal publication?** → Website or blog
**From newspaper with date?** → Newspaper article
**From .gov or official organization?** → Government document or report
