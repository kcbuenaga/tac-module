# Quality Scoring Rubric

**Purpose:** Standardized framework for assessing academic source quality. Loaded by step-04 for consistent quality scoring across all sources.

**Quality Score Calculation:** 0-100% based on three weighted criteria

---

## Quality Score Components

### 1. Peer Review Status (40% of Quality Score)

**Definition:** Has the source undergone formal peer review process?

#### Scoring Rubric:

**100 points (Peer-Reviewed Journal Article)**
- Published in established peer-reviewed academic journal
- Underwent formal blind or double-blind peer review
- Editorial oversight from recognized scholars
- **Examples:** Articles in *Academy of Management Journal*, *JAMA*, *Nature*

**80 points (Peer-Reviewed Conference Proceedings)**
- Presented at academic conference with peer review
- Published in conference proceedings
- Editorial review by program committee
- **Examples:** ICIS, ACM conferences, IEEE symposiums

**60 points (Academic Book / Book Chapter)**
- Published by academic press
- Editorial review (not always peer review)
- Scholarly audience
- **Examples:** Oxford University Press, Cambridge University Press

**50 points (Preprint / Working Paper)**
- Scholarly content but not yet peer-reviewed
- May be under review for journal
- Author credentials from academic institution
- **Examples:** SSRN papers, arXiv preprints, university working papers

**40 points (Thesis / Dissertation)**
- Graduate-level academic work
- Committee review (similar to peer review)
- Original research
- **Examples:** PhD dissertations, master's theses

**30 points (Grey Literature - Professional)**
- Professional reports, white papers
- Industry or government publications
- Editorial oversight but not academic peer review
- **Examples:** McKinsey reports, WHO guidelines, government reports

**20 points (Grey Literature - Other)**
- Blog posts by credentialed experts
- Op-eds in reputable publications
- Professional magazine articles
- **Examples:** *Harvard Business Review* blog, *The Conversation*

**10 points (Unverified)**
- No clear peer review or editorial process
- Self-published or vanity press
- Questionable credibility
- **Use with caution**

#### How to Assess:

1. **Check journal website** for peer review policy
2. **Look for "peer-reviewed" designation** in database
3. **Verify journal legitimacy** (not predatory publisher)
4. **Check publication type** (article, conference, book, report)

**Red Flags (Predatory Journals):**
- Pay-to-publish model with no peer review
- Promises rapid publication (days to weeks)
- Mimics legitimate journal names
- No clear editorial board
- Excessive spam emails soliciting submissions

**Tool to Check:** Beall's List (archived), Think Check Submit, DOAJ for open-access journals

---

### 2. Journal Tier / Publisher Reputation (35% of Quality Score)

**Definition:** What is the academic reputation and impact of the publication venue?

#### Scoring Rubric:

**100 points (Tier 1 - Top Journals in Field)**
- Top 5% of journals in specific discipline
- High impact factor (IF) relative to field
- Highly selective acceptance rate (<15%)
- Recognized globally as premier outlet
- **How to Identify:**
  - Check Scimago Journal Rank (SJR) - Q1 ranking
  - Web of Science Journal Impact Factor - top quartile
  - ABDC Journal Quality List - A* rating
  - Ask field experts or advisors

**Examples by Field:**
- Management: *Academy of Management Journal*, *Administrative Science Quarterly*
- Healthcare: *The Lancet*, *JAMA*, *New England Journal of Medicine*
- IS/Technology: *MIS Quarterly*, *Information Systems Research*
- Finance: *Journal of Finance*, *Review of Financial Studies*

**85 points (Tier 1 - Top Publisher / University Press)**
- Academic book from prestigious university press
- Top publisher in discipline
- **Examples:** MIT Press, Oxford, Cambridge, Stanford University Press

**75 points (Tier 2 - Established Journals)**
- Well-regarded journals in field
- Solid impact factor (IF) but not top tier
- Acceptance rate 15-30%
- Recognized regionally or by subdiscipline
- **How to Identify:**
  - Scimago SJR - Q1 or Q2 ranking
  - ABDC List - A or B rating
  - Regular citations in top-tier journals

**Examples:**
- *Journal of Business Research*, *Organization Science*, *BMJ Open*

**50 points (Tier 3 - Emerging or Niche Journals)**
- Newer journals building reputation
- Niche focus on specific subtopic
- Lower impact factor but growing
- Legitimate peer review process
- **How to Identify:**
  - Scimago SJR - Q3 ranking
  - ABDC List - C rating
  - Indexed in major databases (Web of Science, Scopus)

**40 points (Tier 3 - Established Publisher, Lower Tier)**
- Published by known academic publisher
- Less selective or niche audience
- **Examples:** Specialized Springer or Elsevier journals

**30 points (Tier 4 - Unknown or Regional Journals)**
- Limited international recognition
- Regional or local focus
- Not indexed in major databases
- Legitimate but lower visibility
- **Verify:** Not a predatory journal

**20 points (Conference Proceedings / Working Papers)**
- Peer-reviewed but not journal publication
- May be stepping stone to journal article
- **Examples:** Academic conference papers

**10 points (Grey Literature / Non-Academic)**
- Professional or industry publications
- Not academic journals
- **Examples:** Trade magazines, think tank reports

**0 points (Predatory or Unverifiable)**
- Cannot verify legitimacy
- Known predatory publisher
- **Exclude from literature review**

#### How to Assess:

1. **Look up journal in Scimago Journal Rank (SJR):**
   - https://www.scimagojr.com/
   - Check quartile ranking (Q1 = top 25%)

2. **Check Web of Science Journal Impact Factor:**
   - Available through institutional access
   - Compare within same field (IF varies by discipline)

3. **Consult ABDC Journal Quality List:**
   - Australian Business Deans Council list
   - Widely used in business/management
   - https://abdc.edu.au/research/abdc-journal-quality-list/

4. **Ask your advisor or field experts**
   - What are the "top journals" in your specific area?

5. **For books: Check publisher reputation**
   - University presses typically high quality
   - Major academic publishers (Springer, Elsevier) variable by series

**Note:** Impact factor varies widely by field. A "high" IF in humanities may be 2.0, while in medicine it may be 10.0+. Always compare within your specific discipline.

### 3. Citation Metrics (25% of Quality Score)

**Definition:** How often has this source been cited by other scholars?

#### Scoring Rubric:

**Citation counts are adjusted by publication age** - recent articles have less time to accumulate citations.

**For Articles Published 5+ Years Ago:**

- **100 points:** 100+ citations (highly influential)
- **90 points:** 50-99 citations (strong impact)
- **75 points:** 20-49 citations (solid impact)
- **60 points:** 10-19 citations (moderate impact)
- **40 points:** 5-9 citations (emerging impact)
- **20 points:** 1-4 citations (limited impact)
- **10 points:** 0 citations (not yet cited)

**For Articles Published 2-5 Years Ago:**

- **100 points:** 50+ citations (exceptional for age)
- **90 points:** 25-49 citations (very strong)
- **75 points:** 10-24 citations (strong)
- **60 points:** 5-9 citations (solid)
- **40 points:** 2-4 citations (moderate)
- **20 points:** 1 citation (emerging)
- **10 points:** 0 citations (too recent)

**For Articles Published <2 Years Ago:**

- **100 points:** 20+ citations (remarkable for age)
- **90 points:** 10-19 citations (very strong)
- **75 points:** 5-9 citations (strong)
- **60 points:** 2-4 citations (solid)
- **40 points:** 1 citation (emerging)
- **20 points:** 0 citations (too recent to assess)

**Special Cases:**

- **Books:** Typically accumulate citations more slowly - use lower thresholds
- **Seminal/Classic Works:** May have 1000+ citations (cap at 100 points)
- **Highly Specialized Topics:** Lower citation counts expected (adjust expectations)
- **Recent Preprints:** 0 citations acceptable if content is cutting-edge

#### How to Assess:

1. **Google Scholar:**
   - Search for article title in quotes
   - "Cited by X" shows citation count
   - Click "Cited by" to see who cited it

2. **Web of Science:**
   - "Times Cited" shows citation count
   - Filter to see only citations from high-quality journals

3. **Scopus:**
   - "Cited by" shows citation count
   - Similar to Web of Science

**Citation Context Matters:**
- High citations from top journals = stronger indicator
- High citations from predatory journals = less meaningful
- Check if highly cited because it's foundational vs. controversial

## Special Considerations

### Non-Traditional Sources

**When Grey Literature is Acceptable:**
- Government reports (WHO, CDC, NIH guidelines)
- Industry standards (ISO, IEEE)
- Reputable think tanks (McKinsey, Brookings Institution)
- NGO reports (World Bank, UN agencies)

**Quality Assessment:**
- Evaluate author credentials and institutional affiliation
- Check citation count in academic literature
- Verify independence and methodology
- Note limitations in your literature review

### Non-English Sources

**Quality indicators remain the same:**
- Peer review status
- Journal reputation (regional or international)
- Citation metrics

**Additional checks:**
- Verify journal indexed in international databases
- Check Scimago for non-English journals
- Evaluate translation quality if using translated version

### Open Access Considerations

**Open Access â‰  Low Quality**
- Many top journals offer open access options (hybrid model)
- Some journals are fully open access and high quality (*PLOS ONE*, *BMJ Open*)
- Check: Directory of Open Access Journals (DOAJ)

**Predatory Open Access:**
- Pay-to-publish with no peer review
- Promises fast publication
- Mimics legitimate journal names
- **Check:** Think Check Submit guidelines
